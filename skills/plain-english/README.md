# 📢 Plain English Please

Give it anything unreadable. Get back what it was actually trying to say.

---

## What it does

Takes jargon, technical language, corporate speak, or anything written to impress rather than communicate — and rewrites it in plain, direct English without losing any meaning.

Calibrated to the audience: plain English for a general reader is different to plain English for a specialist who just needs the padding stripped out. If the audience isn't clear, it asks one quick question first.

If something had no meaning once the complexity was stripped — it'll say so. Sometimes the honest rewrite is: *"This sentence isn't saying anything. Cut it."*

---

## When it fires

**Fires when:**
- You paste in something unreadable and need it translated
- You ask to "simplify this", "make this clearer", or "explain this like I'm not a robot"
- The text uses jargon or technical language that's obscuring rather than communicating

**Does not fire when:**
- The language is already clear and just happens to be long
- Technical terms are genuinely necessary for the audience

---

## Quick example

> **You:** Can you translate this?
> *"The utilisation of a multi-stakeholder engagement framework will enable the facilitation of cross-functional synergies and the optimisation of outcomes across the organisational ecosystem."*
>
> **Claude:** *"Getting different teams to work together should lead to better results."*
>
> Note: the original sentence gestures at something but doesn't actually say what teams, what outcomes, or how. If there's a real claim in there, it needs to be made explicitly — this version is as specific as the original allows.
