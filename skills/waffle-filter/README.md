# 🧹 Waffle Filter

Every buzzword. Every filler phrase. Named, annotated, deleted. Clean version delivered.

---

## What it does

Give it a piece of writing — an email, a bio, a proposal, a LinkedIn post, a report. It finds every offender: the synergies, the leveraging, the going-forwards, the sentences that exist purely to sound like something is being said. Each one gets a line of dry commentary and a fix. Then you get the full clean version.

Particularly unforgiving about LinkedIn energy, startup pitch language, passive voice used to dodge accountability, and anything that uses "synergistic" unironically.

If something had no meaning once the waffle was stripped, it'll say so: *"This sentence doesn't have a plain English version because it isn't saying anything. Cut it."*

---

## When it fires

**Fires when:**
- You share a piece of writing padded with buzzwords, filler, or corporate speak
- You ask Claude to review writing for clarity or tightness
- The writing exists to sound impressive rather than to communicate

**Does not fire when:**
- The writing is actually clear and just happens to be long
- Technical language is genuinely necessary for the audience

---

## Quick example

> **You:** Can you review this?
> *"We are excited to leverage our synergistic partnership opportunities to deliver innovative, best-in-class solutions that drive value across the ecosystem going forward."*
>
> **Claude:**
> - *"synergistic partnership opportunities"* — synergistic means nothing here. "Partnership opportunities" already does the job.
> - *"leverage"* — use.
> - *"innovative, best-in-class solutions"* — says who? Cut both.
> - *"drive value across the ecosystem"* — the ecosystem of what? Cut or be specific.
> - *"going forward"* — always cut.
>
> **Clean version:**
> *"We're excited to work with partners to build things that actually help people."*
>
> Or, if you know what the solution does: lead with that.
