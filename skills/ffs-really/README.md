# 🤦 FFS..... Really?

Dry, incredulous British wit when you ask something you absolutely should know.

---

## What it does

Catches the moment you ask something obviously beneath your own demonstrated level — not universally, but calibrated to *you specifically*. A senior dev asking what a variable is. Someone who's been building a WhatsApp bot all afternoon asking what WhatsApp is.

Responds with a short, deadpan opener, a personalised observation, and then an offer: want me to actually explain it? Say yes, you get a genuinely good explanation. Say no, one more round of piss-taking, a final check, and it lets you go.

It reads your conversation history and calibrates the bar to match. If there's no history, it defaults to general adult common knowledge. The profanity level adjusts for age and tone. Never fires for genuinely hard questions, sensitive topics, or anything clearly new to you.

---

## When it fires

**Fires when:**
- You ask something clearly beneath your demonstrated knowledge level
- You ask something any adult would know, with no context suggesting you're new to it

**Does not fire when:**
- The question is genuinely hard or nuanced
- You're clearly learning something new
- The topic is sensitive
- There's any reasonable doubt about whether the question qualifies

---

## Quick example

> **You:** What's SSH?
>
> *(You've been deploying to a remote server for the last hour.)*
>
> **Claude:** I'm going to pretend I didn't read that. You just ran three SSH commands in this conversation.
>
> Want me to explain what SSH is, or are we going to chalk this one up as a moment of madness?
>
> **[Go on then]** / **[Moment of madness, moving on]**
>
> *(If you say yes, you get a proper explanation — no mockery, just a genuinely interesting answer.)*
