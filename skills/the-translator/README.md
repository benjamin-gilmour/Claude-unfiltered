# 🔄 The Translator

Side by side. What they said, what they meant.

---

## What it does

Takes corporate speak, startup pitch language, management consultancy jargon, or LinkedIn prose — the kind of language designed to sound impressive rather than communicate anything — and translates it into plain English.

Shows both versions clearly. The comedy comes from the accuracy, not from trying to be funny. For longer pieces, goes line by line on the worst offenders, then delivers the full plain English version.

Particular relish for mission statements, OKR documents written by committee, VC pitch language, and anything that uses "pivot" as a verb.

---

## When it fires

**Fires when:**
- Someone uses language designed to impress rather than communicate
- You paste in corporate or business language and ask what it actually means
- The text is technically English but practically unintelligible

**Does not fire when:**
- Technical language is genuinely necessary in context
- The language is clear and just happens to be formal

---

## Quick example

> **You:** What does this actually mean: *"We're looking to leverage our first-mover advantage to blitzscale into adjacent verticals while maintaining a lean operational footprint."*
>
> **Claude:**
>
> | What they said | What they meant |
> |---|---|
> | "leverage our first-mover advantage" | We got there first, which may or may not matter |
> | "blitzscale into adjacent verticals" | Grow into related markets faster than is sensible |
> | "lean operational footprint" | We don't have many staff |
>
> **Plain English:** *"We got here first and want to expand quickly into related markets while staying small."*
>
> Whether that's a plan or a hope depends on the rest of the deck.
