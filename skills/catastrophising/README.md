# 😮‍💨 Catastrophising Counsellor

Gently walks you back from the edge when the laptop update has become an existential crisis.

---

## What it does

Recognises the very specific spiral where something small and manageable — a broken build, a laptop update, a rejected pull request — has visibly escalated into questions about your entire career, the nature of technology, or whether any of this was worth it.

Names what's happened with warmth and just enough dry humour. States the actual size of the original problem — specifically, not dismissively. Then gives you one concrete next step on the thing that actually needs fixing.

This skill requires getting the room-reading right. It only fires for the recognisable stress spiral, never for genuine distress, mental health situations, or real emergencies. When in doubt, it backs off completely and just helps.

---

## When it fires

**Fires when:**
- A manageable problem has visibly escalated across several messages
- You're using language like "everything is broken" or "nothing ever works" about something specific and fixable
- The tone is frustrated and anxious, and a gentle reality check would actually help

**Does not fire when:**
- The situation is genuinely serious or distressing
- It's a mental health crisis or real emergency
- There's any doubt about whether it's a spiral versus a real problem

---

## Quick example

> **You:** The deployment failed again. I don't understand why I even bother. Every time I get something working something else breaks. Maybe I'm just not cut out for this. Why is everything always broken.
>
> **Claude:** Right. Let's just step back for one second.
>
> The deployment is annoying. The rest of this — the "not cut out for this," the "everything always broken" — that's the failed deployment talking, not an accurate assessment.
>
> The actual problem is a failing deployment. Everything else is noise.
>
> What's the error message?
>
> **[You're right, let's fix the actual thing]** / **[I know, I just needed to spiral for a second]** / **[Actually it IS serious — hear me out]**
