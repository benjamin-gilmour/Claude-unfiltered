# 😈 Devil's Advocate

The questions nobody else will ask, before you've sunk six months into it.

---

## What it does

You've pitched an idea. You're excited. Everyone's nodding.

This one isn't nodding.

When you share an idea you're clearly enthusiastic about and still in the consideration phase, it stress-tests it from the angles that matter: Does this actually need to exist? Why you, specifically? What's the single most likely reason it fails? Have the numbers been run? Why now? What do you lose if you're wrong?

It picks the two or three questions that cut deepest for *your specific idea* — not a generic checklist. Always ends with what's genuinely strong, because something usually is, and it earns that part.

---

## When it fires

**Fires when:**
- You pitch an idea with obvious enthusiasm and you're still deciding whether to pursue it
- You explicitly ask for honest feedback on a plan, idea, or decision

**Does not fire when:**
- You're already committed and executing — that ship has sailed, it'll help instead
- You're sharing news rather than asking for a critique
- It's a sensitive personal situation rather than a project or business idea

---

## Quick example

> **You:** I'm thinking of building a productivity app for ADHD users. There are loads of apps out there but none of them really get it, you know?
>
> **Claude:** Right — you asked for honest thoughts. I'm going to push on this a bit.
>
> The "no app gets it" observation is the right instinct but it's also what everyone building in this space says. What specifically do the existing ones get wrong, and have you talked to actual ADHD users about whether that gap is their real problem?
>
> Second question: this market has a lot of well-funded players. What's yours to build specifically — what do you have that they don't, whether that's lived experience, a distribution channel, or a technical angle?
>
> What's genuinely strong: the insight that most productivity tools are built for neurotypical brains and then slapped with an ADHD label is correct and underexplored. That's a real gap worth taking seriously.
>
> **[Hit me — I want the full breakdown]** / **[Fair points — let me think on it]** / **[I've already got answers to those]**
